1. Medical Equipment & Supplies
Check first aid kits are fully stocked
Verify oxygen tanks are full and functional
Inspect AED (Automated External Defibrillator) battery and pads
Confirm trauma kits are sealed and complete
Replace expired medications or supplies
Restock splints, bandages, and cervical collars ---

2. Rescue Gear
Inspect toboggans (sleds) for damage or missing parts
Ensure all toboggans have rescue blankets and straps
Check ropes, carabiners, and belay systems for wear
Verify radio harnesses and packs are equipped and functional
Confirm headlamps and backup batteries are available ---

3. Communication & Radios
Radio check-in at shift start
Test base-to-patrol radio communication
Charge and clean radios after use
Distribute radios with correct frequencies/programs ---

4. Signage & Trail Markings
Inspect open/closed trail signs and boundary ropes
Replace faded or damaged signage
Verify hazard markers (e.g., cliff, ice, thin cover) are in place
Reposition drifted signs or barriers due to wind/snow ---

5. Slope & Terrain Safety
Patrol and assess all open trails for hazards
Remove fallen branches or debris
Mark unmarked obstacles (rocks, holes, ice)
Evaluate snow conditions and log for avalanche risk
Check terrain park features for structural integrity
Confirm lift line fencing and merge lanes are set ---

6. Avalanche Control (if applicable)
Review avalanche forecast and bulletin
Conduct morning beacon check
Confirm avy packs, probes, shovels, and beacons are functional
Log avy control routes and mitigation actions ---

7. Facilities & Base Area
Inspect patrol room cleanliness and supplies
Restock blankets, gloves, and hot packs for emergencies
Confirm signage at base is visible and accurate
Check access to medical transport routes ---

8. Documentation & Reporting
Log shift start time and radio call sign
Record weather and snow conditions
Note any incidents or rescues in daily log
Report any equipment needs or deficiencies
