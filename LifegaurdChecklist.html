LIFEGAURD PRODUCTIVITY


Lifegaurd Daily Checklist






1. Pre-Shift Preparation
Arrive on time and check in with supervisor
Dress in full uniform (including whistle, rescue tube, sunscreen, sunglasses, hat)
Hydrate before shift
Review weather and water conditions (tide, waves, current, visibility)
Confirm emergency equipment is present and functional:
Rescue tubes
First aid kits
AED
Radios / communication devices
Attend daily briefing or team meeting

2. Station Setup
Open lifeguard stand or tower
Set up warning flags, signage, and beach condition notices
Place buoys, lane ropes, or designated swim zone markers (if applicable)
Ensure visibility from post is clear (no obstructions)

3. Active Surveillance
Maintain constant visual scan of assigned area
Rotate positions according to rotation schedule
Enforce safety rules (diving, running, rough play, depth limits)
Educate patrons on hazards (rip currents, deep areas, no-swim zones)
Document and report any incidents or infractions

4. Maintenance and Cleanliness
Inspect and clean lifeguard station or chair
Report damaged or missing equipment
Check pool deck/beach area for hazards (glass, debris, slick surfaces)
Refill first aid supplies if used

5. Mid-Shift & Break Management
Take scheduled hydration and rest breaks
Log out/in for breaks
Communicate hand-off clearly when rotating out

6. Emergency Preparedness
Perform or review emergency drills (monthly or weekly schedule)
Review CPR, spinal injury, and drowning protocols
Test radios and emergency communication devices
Confirm emergency contact numbers are accessible

7. End of Shift Duties
Debrief with supervisor
Record incident reports and log shift activities
Store and secure equipment
Remove or update warning signs if conditions have changed
Clean personal and shared areas
